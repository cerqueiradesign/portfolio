A city in the
mountains deserves
an identity as
layered as its snow.

Covilhã's narrow streets carry 800 years of history, always with Serra da Estrela as backdrop. The identity draws from the mountain landscape and the region's wool industry — Covilhã was historically Portugal's textile capital. The geometric pattern system is directly inspired by Burel, the traditional woven fabric of the Serra.

Challenge

A city with too many
identities — and none
that held them together.

Fortress town, textile capital, university city, mountain gateway — four rich identities that never spoke to each other. The existing visual presence was fragmented, failing to reflect either the depth of its heritage or the ambition of its future.

Strategy

Wool, stone
and altitude.

Three materials anchored every decision: Burel (the geometric pattern system), carved stone lettering (the typography), and the mountain itself (the colour palette — cold blues, snow whites, slate greys).

Positioning: a city that transforms raw material into identity — wool into cloth, stone into architecture, altitude into character.
